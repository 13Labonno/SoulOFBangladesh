The History of Bangladesh Transportation

The Pre-Early Transportation Era

The Early Transportation Era (pre 1800s)

The history of transport in this region, historically Bengal, was dominated by waterways due to the vast network of rivers. Boats were the principal means of carrying goods and people.

Horse Carriages and Early Public Transport 1856

Horse Carriages and Early Public Transport 1856

Horse-driven carriages were formally introduced as the first form of public transport in Dhaka, marking a shift towards more structured city movement.

The Dawn of Rail Transport(1862)

The Dawn of Rail Transport

Rail transport began on November 15, 1862, when the first broad-gauge line, approximately 53 km long, was opened between Darshana (Chuadanga District) and Jogotee (Kushtia District), connecting the region to the larger Indian rail network..

East Bengal Rail Expansion (1895)

East Bengal Expansion

Two important sections of metre-gauge railway were constructed by British companies: one connecting Chattogram and Cumilla and another linking Laksam and Chandpur, primarily for commercial purposes like jute transport.

Bangabandhu Bridge and National Connectivity (1998)

Bangabandhu Bridge and National Connectivity

The completion of the Bangabandhu Jamuna Multipurpose Bridge (Jamuna Bridge) was a landmark event. It established a direct road and rail link between the eastern and western parts of the country, massively improving national connectivity and trade.

Maitree Express Revival(2009)

Maitree Express Revival

Rail transport between Bangladesh and India was renewed with the launch of the Maitree Express, re-establishing a diplomatic and cultural link after 43 years.

Dhaka Mawa Bhnaga Expressway(2020)

Dhaka Mawa Bhanga Expressway

The Dhaka–Mawa–Bhanga Expressway (later part of the Padma Bridge approach roads) was opened, becoming the country's first modern controlled-access highway, signaling a move towards high-speed road infrastructure.

Padma Bridge and Metro Rail Inauguration (2022)

Padma Bridge and Metro Rail Inauguration

A monumental year. The Padma Multipurpose Bridge was opened, connecting the south-western region directly to the rest of the country by road and future rail. Additionally, the first phase of the Dhaka Metro Rail (MRT Line-6) from Uttara North to Agargaon was inaugurated on December 28, becoming the first electric-powered, elevated metro rail system in Bangladesh.

Metro Rail Expansion (2023)

Metro Rail Expansion

The Dhaka Metro Rail's MRT Line-6 was further extended, with the second phase from Agargaon to Motijheel being inaugurated, significantly increasing the operational route and impact on Dhaka's daily commute.

Fun Facts About Bangladesh Transportation

Colorful Rickshaws

Dhaka is called the “Rickshaw Capital of the World” with over 1 million cycle rickshaws, each decorated with vibrant artwork.

Floating Transport

Bangladesh has one of the largest river networks in the world, and boats are a daily means of transport for millions of people.

Festive Train Journeys

During Eid holidays, trains carry double their capacity as thousands return to their hometowns, making it one of the busiest rail events.

Oldest Airport

Tejgaon Airport in Dhaka was built during World War II in 1943 and is one of the oldest airstrips in South Asia.
